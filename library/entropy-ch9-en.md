## 7. Find Your Child's Combination

<div style="background-color: #fff5f5; padding: 20px; border-radius: 8px; margin-bottom: 15px;">
  <strong style="color: #c53030;">Common Thought (Increase in Entropy)</strong>
  <ul style="margin-top: 10px; margin-bottom: 0; color: #555;">
    <li>"The kid next door benefited from this study method. I should make my child do it too."</li>
    <li>"This is the **study method used by the top student in school**. You must do it this way too."</li>
  </ul>
</div>

<div style="background-color: #f0fdf4; padding: 20px; border-radius: 8px; margin-bottom: 15px;">
  <strong style="color: #2f855a;">Rethink (Building Order)</strong><br><br>
  <span style="color: #333;">There is no perfect study method in the world. What might be a panacea for others can be just **'confusion'** for your child if it doesn't suit them, rather than knowledge.</span><br><br>
  <span style="color: #333;">What we need is a **'Modular Study Method'**. Just like assembling LEGO blocks, try combining methods that suit your child.</span>
  <br><br>
  <ul style="color: #555;">
    <li>For **concept understanding**, use visual materials (Method A).</li>
    <li>For **problem-solving**, utilize a timer (Method B).</li>
    <li>For **review**, use verbalization (Method C).</li>
  </ul>
  <span style="color: #333;">Trying various methods is not a failure. It's a **'data collection process'** to deeply understand your child and a journey to find the optimal order.</span>
</div>

<div style="background-color: #fffbeb; padding: 20px; border-radius: 8px; margin-bottom: 30px;">
  <strong style="color: #b45309;">Core Principle</strong><br><br>
  <span style="color: #333;">Ask, "How does this method feel to you?" The **'combination unique to your child'** discovered together through dialogue becomes the most powerful weapon.</span>
</div>

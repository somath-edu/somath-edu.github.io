**[Opinion] The Physical Self-Awareness of Artificial Intelligence: Changing Paradigms of Labor and Existence**

The central focus of the information technology (IT) industry is rapidly shifting from "Generative AI" to "Physical AI." While Large Language Models (LLMs) have previously functioned as a "brain" by learning human language and knowledge, we are now entering a stage where this intelligence is transplanted into a physical entity, granting it a "body" that can move and perform tasks directly. The dramatic advancement of humanoid robots that resemble the human form, such as Tesla’s "Optimus" and Figure AI’s "Figure 01," signals a massive wave of change that goes beyond mere technological progress to affect the entire socio-economic structure.

The core of Physical AI lies in "Embodied AI." While traditional robots only repeated fixed trajectories according to pre-programmed instructions, Physical AI perceives its surroundings in real-time through visual sensors and decides its own movements. A crucial technical challenge in this process is bridging the so-called "Sim-to-Real" gap. This technology, which minimizes the errors that occur when applying data learned in a virtual world to the real world governed by physical laws, serves as the foundation for robots to pick up objects delicately or maintain balance on irregular terrain, just like humans.

These technological shifts are highly likely to shake the foundations of the labor market. While past automation was limited to simple and repetitive industrial labor, intelligent humanoid robots are expanding their reach into "unstructured tasks" that were once believed to be performable only by humans, such as domestic chores, elderly care, and rescue operations at dangerous disaster sites. This represents an opportunity for a dramatic leap in labor productivity, but it simultaneously contains elements of social conflict, such as the displacement of low-skilled labor. Specifically, expectations for robots to act as a "relief pitcher" to solve labor shortages caused by aging populations coexist with fears of them being "invaders" that take away human jobs.

Furthermore, we must confront the ethical and philosophical questions that arise when artificial intelligence possesses a "body." Human intelligence is formed through physical experiences and sensory interactions. If AI begins to sense and experience the world through a physical body, will the "texture" of the intelligence they develop become similar to that of humans? Also, when robots begin to mimic human-like physical pain or physical limitations, what kind of legal and ethical status should we grant them?

Ultimately, the emergence of Physical AI and humanoids forces us to redefine the very meaning of human existence beyond the evolution of tools. Technology must remain a tool that expands human limits, but there is an urgent need for sophisticated social consensus and institutional frameworks to ensure that these tools do not threaten human uniqueness. We are standing on the threshold of a monumental era where we must coexist with machines that possess both intelligence and a physical body.

**[Editorial] The Paradigm Shift in AI: How ‘Thinking Machines’ Are Reshaping Our Society**

The most prominent topic in the information and communication technology (ICT) industry today is the enhancement of “reasoning capabilities” in artificial intelligence (AI). While conventional Large Language Models (LLMs), represented by ChatGPT, have primarily focused on “probabilistic generation”—predicting the next word based on vast datasets—they are now entering a stage of “logical reasoning,” where they analyze problems step-by-step to find solutions much like humans do. This shift represents more than just technical progress; it is a fundamental transformation that reshapes industrial structures and the very definition of human intellectual labor.

Existing generative AI was optimized for providing instantaneous answers to questions. However, this process closely resembles “System 1” of the human cognitive framework proposed by Daniel Kahneman, which is characterized by intuitive and rapid judgment. In contrast, recently emerging advanced models mimic the deliberate and logical thought processes of “System 2.” Before producing an output, these models form an internal “Chain of Thought,” allowing them to verify their own logical errors and solve complex mathematical or coding problems through sequential steps.

The emergence of such “reasoning AI” carries significant economic weight, specifically regarding the “efficiency of reasoning costs.” This implies that tasks previously reserved for highly skilled experts—such as complex data analysis, legal reviews, and the discovery of new drug candidates—can now be performed by AI at a substantially lower cost. While this provides an opportunity to exponentially increase productivity in knowledge-based industries, it simultaneously demands a redefinition of the roles of high-value professionals. Human competitiveness is no longer about “knowing the answer” but is shifting toward the “ability to define and verify problems” so that AI can perform accurate reasoning.

From a social perspective, it is expected that AI’s “hallucination” phenomenon will be drastically reduced. This is because reasoning AI, which undergoes a process of logical verification, can independently assess the consistency of facts. At the same time, however, the “black box” problem—the difficulty humans face in fully understanding the internal derivation of an AI’s conclusion—may intensify. As AI-generated logical conclusions potentially conflict with ethical values, there is an urgent need for social consensus on how we will regulate these processes and who will be held accountable.

Ultimately, the evolution of AI poses a fundamental question: In an era where machines encroach upon the realm of “thought,” once considered a unique human domain, what constitutes human intelligence? In both education and industry, the ability to organically grasp complex situations and maintain critical thinking has become more vital than ever. We stand at a crossroads where we must cultivate our capacity as “conductors” who direct the powerful instrument known as artificial intelligence.

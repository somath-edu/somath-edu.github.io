Recent discussions in the global IT industry have centered on the enhancement of “reasoning capabilities” in Artificial Intelligence (AI). While Large Language Models (LLMs), such as ChatGPT, have hitherto focused on “pattern matching”—probabilistically predicting the next word based on vast datasets—a new era of “thinking AI” is emerging. This new generation of AI, much like humans, contemplates deeply and verifies logical steps independently. This transition signifies more than a mere performance boost; it represents a fundamental shift in the underlying mechanism of how AI solves problems.

Psychologist Daniel Kahneman categorized human thought processes into two systems: “System 1,” which is intuitive and fast, and “System 2,” which is logical and deliberate. Conventional LLMs have primarily relied on System 1. Although they provide instantaneous responses, they often suffer from “hallucinations,” producing nonsensical answers to complex mathematical or highly structured logical problems. This occurs because the models have lacked an internal verification process to scrutinize their own outputs.

Newly developed “Reasoning AI” seeks innovation at this exact juncture by employing a process known as the “Chain of Thought.” These models break a problem down into smaller units and spend time thinking, self-correcting any logical errors at each stage. In essence, it is an attempt to implement System 2 by granting AI “time to think.” This suggests that the conventional belief—that intelligence increases proportionally with data volume (the Scaling Law)—has reached its limit, and it reflects a strategic choice to maximize the efficiency of computational resources.

This shift also marks a significant inflection point in economic and industrial terms. In the past, enormous computing power was consumed during the “training” phase of model development; however, the proportion of computation occurring during the “inference” phase—the stage where AI answers user queries—is now rapidly increasing. This transition is expected not only to reconfigure the demand structure of the semiconductor market but also to establish energy efficiency as a core metric of AI competitiveness.

However, technological progress does not guarantee a bright future alone. As AI acquires logical reasoning skills, the value of critical thinking and professional judgment—long considered the exclusive domains of humans—will be put to the test. Furthermore, concerns are rising that the process through which AI constructs its own logic may become a “black box,” making it difficult for humans to fully control or understand the underlying reasoning.

We are moving beyond the era of “what AI knows” and into the era of “how AI thinks.” In this massive tide of change, what matters most is not the speed of technology, but its direction. Now more than ever, a social discourse is urgently needed to determine how humans will interpret and utilize the logical outputs of AI, and how we can prevent human alienation in the process.

### [오늘의 핵심 영단어]

1. **Paradigm Shift** (인식 체계의 변화 / 패러다임의 전환)
   - A fundamental change in approach or underlying assumptions.
2. **Hallucination** (환각 현상 / AI의 정보 왜곡)
   - In the context of AI, a phenomenon where the model generates false or illogical information confidently.
3. **Inference** (추론)
   - The process of reaching a conclusion based on evidence and reasoning; in AI, the stage where a trained model responds to input.
4. **Inflection Point** (변곡점)
   - A time of significant change in a situation; a turning point.
5. **Discourse** (담론 / 공론)
   - Written or spoken communication or debate, especially formal discussion on a particular topic.

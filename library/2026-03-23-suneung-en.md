### [Opinion] The Twilight of the Search Era and the Dawn of the “Answer” Era: Light and Shadow of Generative AI Search

Humanity’s pursuit of knowledge has reached a significant turning point / as the era of “keyword search,” / which has dominated the internet for the past two decades, / is rapidly giving way to the era of “generative search.” / In this new paradigm, / artificial intelligence (AI) perceives the intent behind a user's question / and directly provides a synthesized answer. / Recent developments, / such as Google’s introduction of “AI Overviews,” the rapid growth of Perplexity, and OpenAI’s announcement of “SearchGPT,” / represent more than mere technological competition; / they are seismic events / that subvert the very foundations of how we consume information.

Traditional search engines function / by listing relevant webpages based on specific keywords / entered by the user. / In this model, / the user acts as an autonomous agent / who must click through various links / to collect and evaluate information. / However, generative AI search utilizes Large Language Models (LLMs) / to navigate vast amounts of web data in real time / and provide a logically summarized, single response. / Consequently, users no longer need to exert the effort of "searching," / because AI "delivers" completed knowledge / by assembling scattered fragments of information.

While this shift is positive / in that it maximizes the efficiency of information access / and allows users to obtain context-aware answers to complex queries, / serious logical traps lie beneath this efficiency / that threaten the health of the information ecosystem.

The most immediate concern involves “data free-riding” and copyright disputes. / Generative AI generates answers / by learning from high-quality content produced by news outlets, blogs, and communities. / While traditional search environments directed traffic back to original sources, / the “zero-click” environment created by AI / fundamentally blocks the opportunity for original creators to generate revenue. / If knowledge producers are not properly compensated, / the supply of quality data will eventually dry up, / leading to a “desertification of information” / where the AI models themselves may ultimately collapse.

Furthermore, the phenomena of information bias and “hallucination” / highlight the increasing importance of critical thinking. / AI generates what is statistically the most plausible answer, / but it does not guarantee that the response is the absolute truth. / In the process of selecting and summarizing information, / there is a high possibility / that specific perspectives are reinforced or unintended distortions occur. / As users become accustomed to the convenience of AI / and accept information uncritically without verifying sources, / social confirmation bias is bound to intensify.

Technological progress always brings convenience, / but we must reflect on what we might be losing in exchange. / The “answers” provided by AI / should not be the end of knowledge / but rather the beginning of thought. / The space once occupied by the labor of typing keywords / must now be filled with sharp insight / to discern the truth and understand the context. / Digital literacy has now evolved / from a mere virtue into a vital tool for survival.

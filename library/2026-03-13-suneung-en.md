**[Senior Correspondent’s Perspective] The Paradigm Shift in Information Navigation and Its Underlying Logic**

For the past several decades, the manner in which humanity consumes information has heavily relied on the act of "searching." In this process, the subject who entered keywords into search engines and selected the most optimal information from a listed array of links was always the human. However, as generative AI technology, led by ChatGPT, integrates with search systems, we stand at a massive turning point—moving from an "era of finding" to an "era of being presented with answers." So-called "AI search," represented by platforms like Perplexity and SearchGPT, is signaling structural changes across the entire knowledge ecosystem, moving beyond mere technological advancement.

The core mechanism of AI search is based on Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) technology. While traditional search engines were limited to providing relevant web addresses from indexed pages, AI search collects data in real-time to generate a "completed answer" that fits the context of the user’s question. From the user's perspective, the inconvenience of clicking through numerous websites one by one disappears; however, this simultaneously serves as a catalyst for intensifying long-standing conflicts between content producers, who are the sources of information, and the platforms themselves.

From an economic perspective, AI search raises concerns regarding the "end of traffic." The traditional search ecosystem was structured to generate advertising revenue in exchange for directing users to original sites. Yet, if AI provides summarized information directly, users have little reason to visit original articles or blogs. This could collapse the revenue models of media outlets and creators who produce high-quality information, and in the long run, it risks causing a "data drought" where the very data AI needs to learn from becomes exhausted. The current global trends of copyright lawsuits and licensing agreements are reflections of this sense of crisis.

Reflection on the cognitive aspect is also required. AI search has a strong tendency to present a single "correct" answer, which is highly likely to undermine the pluralism of information. As search results become more refined, users lose the opportunity to critically examine information or compare diverse perspectives. Furthermore, the "hallucination phenomenon"—a chronic problem of AI—can lead to the distortion of facts. The higher the logical completeness of an answer, the greater the risk that users will accept the information without recognizing its underlying errors.

Ultimately, the evolution of AI search demands a new form of "literacy" from us. The ability to think critically—to verify the sources of information hidden behind the convenience provided by technology and to identify flaws in the logic generated by AI—has become more important than ever. In an age where fragmented information pours down upon us, will we settle for the "answers" thrown at us by machines, or will we remain "questioners" who trace the grounds of those answers? The new tool of AI search can either be a poison that encourages intellectual laziness or a powerful weapon that expands the horizons of knowledge. The choice depends not on the technology, but on the attitude of the humans who consume the information.

### **Today’s Key Vocabulary**

1. **Paradigm (인식의 체계/패러다임):** A typical example or pattern of something; a distinct set of concepts or thought patterns.
2. **Mechanism (기제/구조):** A system of parts working together in a machine; a process by which something takes place.
3. **Pluralism (다원주의/다양성):** A condition or system in which two or more states, groups, principles, or sources of authority coexist.
4. **Hallucination (환각/근거 없는 오류):** In AI context, a phenomenon where the model generates false or misleading information as if it were a fact.
5. **Literacy (문해력/활용 능력):** The ability to read and write; or, the competence or knowledge in a specified area (e.g., AI literacy).

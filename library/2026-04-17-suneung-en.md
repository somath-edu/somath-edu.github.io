## [Opinion] From Tools to Subjects: Technical and Ethical Implications of the ‘AI Agent’

The most discussed topic in the recent information technology (IT) industry is, without a doubt, the ‘AI Agent.’ While generative AI, such as ChatGPT, has remained a ‘passive tool’ that simply provides answers to user questions, the AI agent aims to be an ‘active subject’ that understands a user’s goals and autonomously creates and executes plans to achieve them. This represents more than just technical progress; it presents a fundamental philosophical question regarding the redefinition of the relationship between humans and machines.

The core mechanism of an AI agent lies in the ‘autonomous loop.’ While traditional AI followed a linear structure of ‘input-process-output,’ an agent decomposes a given goal into sub-tasks, calls upon external tools to perform each task, and modifies its next actions based on feedback from the results. For example, in response to a command like "Optimize my business trip schedule for next week," the agent independently makes a series of sequential decisions: checking the user’s calendar, booking flights and accommodations, and even suggesting outfits based on local weather.

This shift is truly revolutionary in terms of economic efficiency. As human intervention is minimized and high-level cognitive tasks are automated, this transition is expected to drive a dramatic surge in the productivity curve. However, as the autonomy of technology increases, the legal and ethical dilemma of ‘accountability’ deepens. If an AI agent causes financial loss due to a system error during a booking process, or excludes certain options based on a biased algorithm, who should be held responsible? Is it the developer, the service provider, or the user who gave the command?

Furthermore, the proliferation of AI agents demands a fundamental reflection on human ‘freedom of choice.’ The optimized results provided by agents are mostly based on algorithmic efficiency. In this process, humans may accept results while omitting the complex process of deliberation, which raises concerns about weakening human critical thinking and autonomous decision-making capabilities in the long term. This is the point where the question arises: is the freedom we enjoy within the ‘invisible frame’ designed by algorithms truly genuine freedom?

Ultimately, the advent of the AI agent era presents us with two major tasks. Technically, we must advance ‘Explainable AI (XAI)’ technology to transparently disclose and control the decision-making processes of AI; socially, we must preemptively establish legal statuses and responsibility guidelines for the actions of agents. Technology exists for human convenience, but we must not allow it to completely replace ‘judgment’ and ‘responsibility,’ which are unique human domains. This is why we must hold onto the controls even as we equip ourselves with the powerful wings of AI agents.

### [Today’s Core Vocabulary]

1. **Autonomous (자율적인):** Acting independently or having the freedom to do so without external control.
2. **Decompose (분해하다):** To break down a complex problem or substance into smaller, simpler parts.
3. **Accountability (책임, 책무):** The fact or condition of being required to explain or justify one’s actions or decisions.
4. **Proliferation (확산, 급증):** A rapid increase in the number or amount of something.
5. **Preemptively (선제적으로):** Done to stop something from happening before it occurs; acting in advance.

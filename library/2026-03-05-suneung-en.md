Recently, the paradigm of artificial intelligence (AI) technology has been rapidly shifting / from the stage of "conversation" through Large Language Models (LLMs) / to the era of "AI Agents" / that perceive user intentions and perform tasks independently. While conventional chatbots were limited to generating appropriate text responses to user questions, / AI agents are characterized by autonomy / as they establish plans, select appropriate tools, and complete execution / to achieve complex goals. This suggests that AI is evolving beyond simple information retrieval and organization / into an "acting intelligence" / that represents practical human tasks and daily life.

The operational principle of AI agents is explained by the combination of "reasoning" and "tool utilization." When a user gives a complex command / such as "Book accommodations and flights within a budget of 1.5 million won for a family trip to Jeju Island next week and create an itinerary," / the agent decomposes this into detailed sub-tasks. Subsequently, it accesses real-time flight reservation systems, accommodation platforms, and weather information APIs / to collect and comparatively analyze data. During this process, the agent undergoes a "Chain of Thought" process / and makes decisions independently / to derive optimal results. It moves beyond simple data learning / to interact with the external environment / and produce substantial physical and digital outcomes.

This change is causing a massive ripple effect in the industrial structure and economic ecosystem. Big tech companies are paying attention to the possibility / that the existing app-based ecosystem may be replaced by AI agents. If an "OS-centric AI" environment is established / where an agent integrates and executes the functions of all apps / instead of the method of running individual apps one by one, / the power landscape of platforms will inevitably be completely reorganized. Furthermore, from a corporate perspective, / a dramatic improvement in productivity is expected / as AI agents are deployed in areas ranging from simple repetitive administrative tasks to data analysis requiring high-level judgment. However, this simultaneously demands a fundamental redefinition of the value of human labor and the nature of jobs, / and also acts as a factor that amplifies uncertainty in the employment market.

However, the proliferation of AI agents is faced with numerous technical and ethical challenges / that must be resolved. The biggest issues are the location of "reliability" and "accountability." If AI makes a wrong judgment due to a "hallucination" phenomenon / in the process of acting on behalf of a user’s financial payments or sensitive information management, / social consensus is still lacking regarding / to whom the resulting economic loss or legal responsibility should be attributed. Additionally, the vast collection of personal information that occurs / while the agent learns user preferences / causes issues of privacy invasion and security vulnerabilities.

Ultimately, AI agents are placing us before the question / of whether they will become powerful tools to expand human capabilities / or dangerous entities beyond human control. While enjoying the benefits brought by technological progress, / human "digital literacy"—the ability to critically reflect on and monitor the logical structure of decisions made by AI—is more desperate than ever. We must now go beyond learning how to coexist with AI / and seriously contemplate how to define and manage AI autonomy / within the framework of human value systems.

1. **Autonomous** (자율적인): Acting independently or having the freedom to do so.
2. **Decompose** (분해하다): To break down a complex problem or substance into smaller parts.
3. **Reorganize** (재편하다/개조하다): To change the way in which something is organized or structured.
4. **Hallucination** (환각/환각 현상): In AI, a phenomenon where the model generates false or misleading information.
5. **Literacy** (문해력/능력): The ability to read and write, or specialized knowledge in a specific area (e.g., digital literacy).

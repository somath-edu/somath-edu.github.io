**[Introduction: A Paradigm Shift in Artificial Intelligence]**

The most prominent discourse in the contemporary information technology (IT) industry is, without a doubt, the "AI Agent." While the generative artificial intelligence that captivated the public over the past two years, such as ChatGPT, remained at the level of a "language model" responding to user queries, it is now evolving into an "acting intelligence" that autonomously establishes plans and completes executions. This transition signifies that we have entered a new industrial phase where AI moves beyond simply searching for and summarizing information to managing complex human work processes.

**[Body 1: From Reasoning to Execution, the Essence of Agentic Workflow]**

The decisive difference that distinguishes AI agents from conventional generative AI lies in "autonomy" and "tool-use capability." Whereas existing models were passive structures reacting to one-time prompts, agent systems follow what is known as an "Agentic Workflow." This involves an iterative loop in which the AI decomposes a goal into smaller tasks, selects the necessary tools, and performs "self-reflection" to revise its own results if the outcome is unsatisfactory. 

For instance, in response to a request to "plan an overseas trip," while previous AI merely listed information, an agent performs a series of "actions"—booking real-time flights based on the user’s budget and preferences, checking accommodation availability, and connecting to the payment stage. In this process, AI becomes an entity that handles physical and digital tasks by interacting with external APIs (Application Programming Interfaces), transcending its role as a mere calculator.

**[Body 2: Restructuring the Economy and Strategic Corporate Shifts]**

These technological advancements are creating a massive ripple effect across all industries. Big Tech companies are shifting away from simple competition over model performance and are now staking their survival on how effectively they can integrate powerful agent functions into their operating systems (OS) or platforms. This is escalating into a struggle for "platform supremacy," reminiscent of Windows in the PC era and App Stores in the mobile era.

From a corporate perspective, the advantage lies in the efficient allocation of human resources. Structure will be reorganized so that AI agents handle repetitive and standardized tasks, while humans focus on more creative and strategic decision-making. However, this simultaneously foreshadows a rapid transformation in the white-collar labor market and presents new legal and ethical challenges regarding the accountability for decisions made by AI.

**[Conclusion: Critical Acceptance and Defining the Human Role]**

As the era of AI agents arrives, the point we must focus on is not the omnipotence of technology, but rather the "critical thinking" of the humans who operate it. Errors that occur during the AI's autonomous reasoning and execution processes, such as "hallucinations," remain unresolved tasks. Therefore, the ability to verify the final results derived by agents and to act as a "supervisor" to ensure that AI autonomy does not deviate from ethical boundaries will become a core competency for future talent.

Ultimately, an AI agent should be defined not as a being that replaces humans, but as a powerful intelligent tool that extends human will. It is time for us to begin serious reflection on how we will collaborate with this new technological companion.

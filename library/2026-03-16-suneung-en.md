# The Dawn of the AI Agent Era: Shifting from Tools to Autonomous Entities

**[Introduction]**
Recent discourse in the information technology (IT) sector has transcended mere "generative AI" and moved toward the emergence of "AI agents." While past artificial intelligence remained a "passive tool" that provided answers to user questions, it is now evolving into an "autonomous subject" that sets its own goals and executes complex procedures. As global big tech companies race to announce a restructuring of the ecosystem centered on AI agents, this heralds a fundamental change not only in technological progress but also in industrial structures and the way humans work.

**[Definition and Principles of AI Agents]**
An AI agent refers to an intelligent system that interprets abstract user commands, autonomously establishes detailed plans to achieve them, and completes execution by directly utilizing necessary tools. While existing Large Language Models (LLMs) focused on the role of a "reasoning engine" predicting the next word in a text, agents combine this with three core elements: planning, memory, and tool use. For instance, if given a command to "book a family trip to Jeju Island next week," the agent checks the family’s schedule and preferences (memory), searches for flights and accommodations (tool use), and designs the optimal itinerary (planning) to complete the task up to the final payment stage.

**[On-device AI and Security]**
"On-device AI" is gaining prominence as a critical infrastructure for realizing such agent technology. Due to the nature of agents that must analyze a user's private data—such as messages, schedules, and photos—technology that processes data on the device itself without transmitting it to the cloud is essential for privacy protection. Recently announced next-generation operating systems by Apple and Samsung focus on providing personalized services while enhancing security by combining internal device data with AI agents. This suggests that AI technology is transforming from "general-purpose intelligence" into "personalized intelligence."

**[Economic Impact and Ethical Challenges]**
The proliferation of AI agents is expected to bring about an increase in overall economic productivity. The so-called "agent-based workflow" allows humans to focus on more creative and high-level decision-making by automating repetitive and exhaustive administrative tasks. However, certain adverse effects cannot be overlooked. Urgent social tasks include the "responsibility gap" problem, where the accountability for accidents caused by an agent's decision becomes unclear, and the labor market imbalance resulting from agents replacing human jobs. Furthermore, the risk of "hallucination"—where agents act autonomously based on false information or learn the user's biases—still persists.

**[Conclusion]**
AI agents are no longer just stories from movies but are becoming the standard for our daily lives and industries. As technology moves beyond assisting human intelligence to becoming the subject of execution, we must establish new standards for how to cooperate with and control these agents. Critical thinking and ethical judgment are domains that AI cannot replace. Therefore, competitiveness in the upcoming agent era will depend not on the technology itself, but on unique human insight that can lead that technology in the right direction.

**[Senior Reporter's Insight]**
"In the era of tools, how to use them was important; however, in the era of agents, the 'quality of questions and commands' will become an individual's core competency. To avoid being subordinate to technology, we must equip ourselves with sharper critical literacy."

**[Introduction: Evolution of Generative AI and the Paradigm Shift]**

The current discourse within the information technology (IT) industry is undeniably centered on "AI Agents." While the past year was characterized by the era of "generative AI" that primarily responded to inquiries, this year marks a transition into the era of "active AI," which independently judges and acts to complete complex tasks. Whereas conventional chatbots remained at the level of generating text or images in response to user prompts, AI agents exhibit a fundamental difference in that they understand the user's ultimate objective, voluntarily design the sub-tasks necessary to achieve it, and utilize external tools to actually execute the work.

**[Body 1: Operational Principles and Autonomy of AI Agents]**

The core competencies of AI agents lie in their "autonomy" and "ability to utilize tools." This framework closely mirrors the "reasoning-planning-execution" structure found in cognitive psychology. Using Large Language Models (LLMs) as their "brain," these agents analyze complex instructions and decompose them into smaller, manageable units of work. Subsequently, the agent performs practical tasks by calling external APIs, such as browsing the web, sending emails, or accessing databases. A particularly noteworthy feature is the "feedback loop." If an error occurs during execution, the agent demonstrates sophisticated logical judgment by analyzing the cause and recalibrating its path independently. This serves as a driving force that enhances the precision of results while minimizing human intervention.

**[Body 2: Economic Ripple Effects and Changes in Labor Structure]**

The universalization of AI agents is projected to exert a profound influence on the labor market and economic structures. This is because AI can now assist in or even replace professional tasks that require a certain level of judgment, going beyond simple repetitive labor. From a corporate perspective, this enables the efficient allocation of human resources and maximizes added value through the provision of hyper-personalized services. On the other hand, the pressure to restructure the job competencies of white-collar workers will intensify. The ability required for humans is shifting from mere knowledge acquisition to "meta-cognition" and "directing capabilities"—the capacity to set sophisticated goals for AI agents and critically evaluate their outputs to make final decisions.

**[Body 3: Ethical Issues and the Locus of Responsibility]**

Behind this technological progress, numerous challenges remain to be resolved. The most pressing issue is the "locus of responsibility." When an autonomous AI agent infringes upon copyrights or incurs financial losses during its operation, legal and ethical standards regarding whether to hold the developer or the user accountable remain ambiguous. Furthermore, security vulnerabilities and privacy concerns that may arise while AI directly accesses external systems to process information cannot be ignored. A societal consensus on the extent of authority granted to AI agents is urgently required at this juncture.

**[Conclusion: Insights for Coexistence between Humans and AI]**

Ultimately, the era of AI agents poses a question regarding inherently human values alongside technological convenience. When technology acts as the hands and feet of humanity, what should humans contemplate, and where should we head? We must not perceive AI agents merely as tools; instead, we should understand their nature as "intelligent companions" and cultivate critical thinking skills to control and collaborate with them. In a rapidly changing technological environment, literacy and logical judgment that penetrate the essence of matters will serve as the final bastion of humanity, ensuring we are not overwhelmed by technology.

### [오늘의 핵심 영단어]

1. **Paradigm shift** (패러다임의 전환): 한 시대의 지배적인 사고방식이나 인식의 틀이 근본적으로 변하는 것.
2. **Autonomy** (자율성): 외부의 간섭 없이 스스로의 원칙에 따라 판단하고 행동하는 성질.
3. **Competency** (역량/능력): 어떤 일을 수행하는 데 필요한 실질적인 지식, 기술, 태도.
4. **Ambiguous** (모호한): 여러 가지 해석이 가능하여 의미나 경계가 분명하지 않은.
5. **Bastion** (보루): 최후까지 지켜야 할 중요한 곳이나 가치.

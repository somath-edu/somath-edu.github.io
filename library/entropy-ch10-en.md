## 3. Deok (Âæ∑): The Power of Relationships

Here, 'Deok' does not mean being a good child.<br>In the context of studying, Deok signifies the **'ability to connect healthily with others'**.

![Power of Relationships Image](/images/IMG_2513.webp)

### Doing It Alone Leads to Ruin: The Tragedy of Isolation

According to the laws of entropy in physics, a system isolated from the outside inevitably becomes disordered. The same applies to studying.

<div style="background-color: #fff5f5; padding: 20px; border-radius: 8px; margin-bottom: 15px;">
  <strong style="color: #c53030;">A child who closes their door and isolates themselves in their own world:</strong>
  <ul style="margin-top: 10px; margin-bottom: 0; color: #555;">
    <li>Does not know what they are misunderstanding</li>
    <li>Fails to discover logical errors in their thinking</li>
    <li>Accumulates incorrect knowledge</li>
  </ul>
</div>

This is the **biggest pitfall of studying alone**.

### Relationships Correct Errors: The Miracle of Openness

Conversely, a child's brain that continuously communicates with friends, teachers, and parents is an 'open system'.

<div style="background-color: #f0fdf4; padding: 20px; border-radius: 8px; margin-bottom: 15px;">
  <strong style="color: #2f855a;">üí¨ Purification Effect Created by Relationships</strong>
  <ul style="margin-top: 10px; margin-bottom: 0; color: #555;">
    <li><strong>Questions and Conversations</strong>: 'External energy' in the form of feedback from others enters and corrects errors in one's thinking.</li>
    <li><strong>Emotional Stability</strong>: Empathy and support, such as "It's okay," act as a coolant, calming an overheated brain.</li>
  </ul>
</div>

When communicating with people, the brain does not become isolated but continuously circulates and organizes itself. This is the principle by which 'Deok (relationships)' develops a learning mind.

<div style="background-color: #fffbeb; padding: 20px; border-radius: 8px; margin-bottom: 30px;">
  <strong style="color: #b45309;">Relationship with Parents: The Most Important Safe Haven</strong><br><br>
  <span style="color: #333;">The most crucial relationship is with parents. A child's brain, anxious about their parents' reactions, is in an 'emergency state'.<br>
  When survival instincts are activated, rational thought (frontal lobe) becomes paralyzed. <strong>Grades flourish only on the foundation of trust between parents and children.</strong></span>
</div>

## 4. Ji (Êô∫): The Power of Understanding

If energy is secured through Che (body) and errors are filtered out through Deok (relationships), only then is **true wisdom** finally achieved.

Here, Ji (Êô∫) is not mere knowledge (what has been memorized). It refers to the state of **'wisdom (what has been understood)'**, where information is connected and organized, ready to be utilized at any time.

![Wisdom Image](/images/IMG_2514.webp)

*   **Knowledge**: Scattered information found on the internet (disorder)
*   **Wisdom**: Insights connected and organized within one's mind (order)

Only knowledge accumulated within a healthy body and open relationships becomes true competence that does not vanish after exams.

If a child is struggling with their studies, instead of changing problem sets, one must **re-evaluate the order**.

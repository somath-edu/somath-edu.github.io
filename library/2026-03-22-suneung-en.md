# From Tools to Proxies: The Evolutionary Shift in the AI Ecosystem

The central focus of the global IT industry has recently undergone a rapid transition, moving beyond mere "generative AI" toward "AI Agents"—systems capable of autonomously executing complex tasks by grasping a user’s underlying intent. While earlier iterations of AI functioned primarily as "intelligent dictionaries" that provided answers to human inquiries, contemporary AI is entering the realm of a "proxy," performing practical actions such as booking flights, drafting business reports, and controlling household appliances. This transformation represents more than just a technical advancement; it signifies a disruptive innovation that threatens the very foundation of the "platform economy" that has persisted for the past two decades.

The traditional digital ecosystem has long been an "app-centric structure," where users directly access individual applications or websites to utilize services. In this process, search engines or app stores have acted as "gateways" controlling user traffic, thereby securing massive advertising revenues and commission fees. This model, often referred to as the "gateway economy" or "attention economy," is designed to maximize a platform's profit the longer a user stays and the more clicks they generate.

However, the emergence of AI agents threatens to sever this "chain of clicks." For instance, when a user commands an AI agent to "complete a reservation for next week’s trip," the agent directly navigates various services—including accommodation, flights, and car rentals—to produce an optimal result. Throughout this process, the user no longer needs to view advertisements on individual platforms or laboriously navigate multiple apps. Consequently, the initiative over the interface shifts from individual service platforms to the "agent that interprets intent." This phenomenon, known as the "invisibilization of the interface," poses a potentially fatal threat to existing platform giants.

This shift heralds a reorganization of economic value. Core competitiveness is no longer determined by who possesses the most content, but rather by who can most accurately interpret and execute the user’s context. Experts refer to this as the dawn of the "Intent Economy." Yet, this trend does not guarantee a purely optimistic future. Concerns have been raised that if AI agents are monopolized by the algorithms of specific corporations, consumer choice could be covertly distorted. It is impossible to rule out the possibility that the "optimal product" recommended by an agent might, in fact, be the one from a company that paid the highest commission.

Ultimately, the arrival of the AI agent era demands a new level of literacy and critical thinking. As technology becomes a proxy that relieves us of our labor, we are endowed with the responsibility of "supervisors" who must monitor the logic behind decisions and the potential biases of algorithms. The ability to discern the logic of massive capital hidden behind invisible interfaces is the true pinnacle of reading comprehension required for modern individuals living in the advanced AI era.

### [오늘의 핵심 영단어]

1. **Proxy** (대리인, 대행자): A person or thing authorized to act on behalf of another.
2. **Disruptive** (파괴적인, 혼란을 일으키는): Causing radical change in an industry or strategy by introducing a new way of doing things.
3. **Initiative** (주도권, 결단력): The power or opportunity to act or take charge before others do.
4. **Monopolize** (독점하다): To get or have complete control or possession of something.
5. **Distort** (왜곡하다): To pull or twist out of shape; to give a misleading or false account or impression of.

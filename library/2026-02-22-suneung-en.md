### [In-Depth Analysis] Evolution or Extinction: The Information Ecosystem Reorganized by 'Conversational AI'

For a long time, the way humans acquire information has relied on the act of "searching." When a user inputs a specific keyword, search engines list relevant links from countless webpages, and the user then selects and combines appropriate information by themselves. However, with the emergence of conversational Artificial Intelligence (AI) search services—such as SearchGPT or Perplexity—the paradigm of knowledge exploration is fundamentally changing.

The core mechanism of traditional search engines lies in "indexing" and "ranking." These engines crawl websites worldwide to create an index and deliver results to users by ranking them according to specific algorithms. In this process, the final processor of the information was the human being. Conversely, modern AI search utilizes "Retrieval-Augmented Generation" (RAG) technology. This is a method where real-time data is extracted from reliable external sources, and then a Large Language Model (LLM) summarizes and reasons through it to provide a completed answer to the user.

While these changes have drastically increased information accessibility, they simultaneously face structural criticism. The most significant point of contention is the "reliability" and "bias" of the information generated by AI. "Hallucination," where AI states fictional facts in plausible-sounding sentences, remains a challenge yet to be solved. Furthermore, concerns are being raised that the copyrights of original creators may be infringed during the process of AI summarizing information, potentially collapsing the virtuous cycle of the web ecosystem. If the "zero-click" phenomenon—where users only check AI-provided answers without visiting original sites—intensifies, the revenue models of media outlets and bloggers who produce high-quality content will be threatened.

From an economic perspective, the competition for dominance among global big tech companies is intensifying. OpenAI and Microsoft have challenged the stronghold of Google, which has enjoyed a monopoly for over 20 years. In response, various nations are accelerating the construction of "Sovereign AI" to maintain national autonomy. This is because the tools used for information search will determine the flow of the digital advertising market and the direction of data sovereignty.

Ultimately, the proliferation of AI search demands a new level of "digital literacy" from us. Now, "critical thinking"—the ability to discover logical contradictions in AI-generated results and verify the objectivity of sources—has become a core competency of intellectuals, rather than the simple "ability to find" information. In an era where AI assembles knowledge for us, it is time for us to possess the insight to see through what lies behind that assembled knowledge.

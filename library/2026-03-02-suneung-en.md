## [Special Feature] From Answering to Acting: How Agentic AI Redefines Our Interaction with Technology

The most significant keyword in the recent information technology (IT) industry is, without a doubt, “Agentic AI.” While the artificial intelligence fever of last year was concentrated on “Generative AI”—which responds to human inquiries in natural language—the paradigm is now shifting toward the era of “AI Agents” that set their own goals and utilize various tools to complete tasks. This represents more than just a technological advancement; it is fundamentally redefining the way humans and computers interact.

The essence of Agentic AI lies in “autonomy” and “execution.” If conventional Large Language Models (LLMs) served primarily as “reasoning engines” that predicted the next most probable word based on vast data, AI agents take concrete “actions” based on these engines. For instance, in response to a request to "plan a trip to Jeju Island for tomorrow," whereas previous AI was limited to suggesting attractions and restaurants, Agentic AI moves into the realm of “execution” by purchasing flight tickets, reserving accommodations, and communicating with rental car companies while considering the user’s budget and preferences.

This mechanism is made possible by three primary elements. First is “planning,” which is the ability to decompose complex goals into smaller subtasks and determine their priorities. Second is “tool use,” through which the AI exerts influence on the virtual or real world by browsing the web, executing code, or calling external Application Programming Interfaces (APIs). Third is “memory,” which establishes short-term and long-term memory systems to learn from past work history and user feedback, reflecting these insights in future decision-making.

From an economic perspective, the emergence of Agentic AI heralds a revolutionary change in the productivity landscape. If software in the past was a passive tool that operated only according to human instructions, AI agents are becoming “digital labor” that can judge and perform tasks independently. This implies an era where AI leads not only the automation of repetitive office work but also specialized data analysis, the formulation of marketing strategies, and even software development itself. Corporations are seeking to minimize costs and maximize efficiency through these agents, which is expected to lead to a reorganization of the overall industrial structure.

However, behind these rosy technological prospects, sharp concerns also exist. The most significant problem is the risk that arises when “hallucinations” (the phenomenon of generating false information) are transferred to the stage of execution. If an AI executes a financial payment or changes security settings based on incorrect information, the resulting damage is incomparably more fatal than a mere text error. Furthermore, numerous challenges remain to be solved, such as the issue of accountability for autonomous AI decisions and social conflicts resulting from the declining value of human labor.

Ultimately, in the era of Agentic AI, the competence required of humans will shift from the “skill of commanding” to the “skill of verifying and managing.” While AI performs numerous subtasks, humans must fulfill the role of “supervisors” and “architects” who set the overall direction, critically reflect on the AI's output, and provide ethical guidelines. We face the paradox that as the autonomy of technology increases, the insight of the humans holding the reins must become even sharper.

### [Key Vocabulary for Students]
1. **Paradigm (n.)**: A typical example or pattern of something; a distinct set of concepts or thought patterns. (패러다임, 전형적인 양식)
2. **Autonomy (n.)**: The right or condition of self-government; the ability to act independently. (자율성, 자치권)
3. **Decompose (v.)**: To break down a complex entity into smaller, simpler parts. (분해하다, 분석하다)
4. **Accountability (n.)**: The fact or condition of being accountable; responsibility for one's actions. (책임, 의무)
5. **Paradox (n.)**: A seemingly absurd or self-contradictory statement or proposition that when investigated may prove to be well founded or true. (역설, 자기모순적인 상황)
